Book #1

The Unending Game

Author: Vikram Sood

Rating: ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐

Review: It's an amazing book which is entertaining and educational. Be prepared to have your worldview completely shattered.

The Unending Game is a book that delves into the world of espionage and intelligence. It is written by Vikram Sood, a former head of the Research and Analysis Wing (RAW), India's external intelligence agency. The book provides an in-depth look at the world of intelligence and espionage, covering a wide range of topics such as the history of intelligence agencies, the role of intelligence in national security, and the challenges faced by intelligence agencies in the modern world. The book is well-researched and provides a comprehensive overview of the subject, making it a valuable resource for anyone interested in the world of intelligence and espionage.

Customers find the content insightful and well-written. However, opinions are mixed on the writing style, with some finding it wonderful and lucid, while others say it lacks good narration and flow of story.

Book #3

FOR ONE MORE DAY

Author: Mitch Albom

Rating: ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐

Review: This is a very good book. I recommend that everyone should read it at least once in their life. It portrays how the pain of losing someone is universal and does not spare anyone.The pain of losing someone close to us can be unbearable, and not everyone can process it maturely. When Chick Benetto loses his mother to a heart attack, he turns to alcohol. This is quickly followed by him abandoning his family and attempting suicide three times, all triggered by the pain of not being invited to his daughter's wedding.

Book #4

Why I am Hindu

Author: Shashi Tharnoor

Rating: ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐

Review: Dr Shashi Tharoor, the erudite and eloquent author of 16 earlier books, now takes up his pen to express his views on Hinduism. As he says in the introductory note to this book, his purpose is two-fold: to “understand… the extraordinary wisdom and virtues of the faith” and to “show the… intolerant and often violent forms of Hindutva…” According to the author himself, this book is targeted at the English-speaking twenty-first century reader. The book is divided into three sections, each consisting of one or more chapters. The first section is titled “My Hinduism” which contains four chapters. The first and second chapters generally cover the basic tenets and beliefs of the faith.

Author: Yuval Noah Harari

Rating: ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐
